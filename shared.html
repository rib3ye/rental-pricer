    Caveats & externalities

    Estimates are rough — verify before acting

    • Auto-lookup uses state-level heuristics, not address-specific comps. Always verify purchase price against recent comps within 0.5 mi and rent against active listings of comparable units.
    • Interior condition is not factored in. Deferred maintenance can swing rent ±15% and CapEx needs dramatically.
    • This tool assumes long-term leases. Short-term/mid-term rental math (STR/MTR) is entirely different — higher gross, much higher expenses, regulatory risk.

    Tax, insurance & regulatory

    • Property tax reassessment: In many states (CA especially via Prop 13), sale triggers reassessment. Seller's current tax bill is NOT what you'll pay.
    • Special assessments: Mello-Roos (CA), CDD fees (FL), PIDs (TX), and similar bonds can add $1k–$4k+/yr on top of base property tax. Pull the full tax bill before closing.
    • Insurance market: In CA, FL, LA, and parts of TX, carriers have paused new policies or priced coverage aggressively. Get a binding quote early; state-run insurers of last resort are expensive and limited.
    • Flood/wildfire: Check FEMA flood zone (msc.fema.gov) and wildfire risk. Additional policies may be required or prudent.
    • Rent control / just-cause eviction: CA AB 1482, OR SB 608, NY HSTPA, many cities. Verify whether the property is covered; it caps rent growth and restricts terminations.

    Operating cost blind spots

    • CapEx is lumpy: A $12k roof or $8k HVAC hits in one year. The reserve line smooths it on paper only.
    • Eviction cost: A non-paying tenant can take 3–9 months and $5k–$15k in legal/lost rent to remove. Not modeled.
    • Bad-debt loss: Vacancy % covers empty units, not tenants who stop paying. Add 1–2% for realistic collection loss.
    • Make-ready/turnover: Heavy-use or post-eviction turns can cost $3k–$10k+.
    • Insurance deductibles: Often $2.5k–$5k. Small claims come out of pocket.
    • Utilities during vacancy: Water/gas/electric minimums are your expense when no tenant is in place.

    Financing caveats

    • Investment-property loans typically require 20–25% down, price a 0.5–0.75% rate premium vs. owner-occupied, and have stricter DTI + reserve requirements.
    • Closing cost estimate excludes prepaid tax/insurance impounds — those add 2–6 months of each at closing.
    • LLC purchases usually require commercial-loan terms (higher rate, shorter amortization, balloon) instead of 30-yr fixed.
    • Depreciation (27.5-yr SL on building basis) is not modeled here but materially improves after-tax returns.
    • This tool shows pre-tax cash flow. Actual after-tax return depends on marginal rate, depreciation, passive-loss rules, and real-estate-professional status.

    Market & macro risks

    • Property taxes in non-Prop-13 states reassess annually with market value — a boom can blow up your underwriting.
    • Insurance and maintenance can grow faster than rent in any given year, compressing cash flow.
    • Interest-rate environment affects refinance optionality and future exit cap rates.
    • Single-property concentration risk: one bad tenant, one roof failure, one local market downturn can erase years of returns.

    What this report does NOT include

    • Formal inspection, appraisal, title search, or survey.
    • Environmental review (UGTs, lead paint for pre-1978, asbestos, radon, meth contamination).
    • Rent-control status verification for the specific parcel.
    • School district quality, crime stats, walk scores, neighborhood trajectory.
    • Legal/tax advice. Consult a local real estate attorney and CPA.

    Security notes on this tool

    • Shared data is stored in a public GitHub Gist. Anyone on the internet who knows the gist ID can read the raw JSON — including the password hash. The password gate is a UI barrier only, not cryptographic protection.
    • The GitHub PAT travels in the URL hash fragment. Treat the share link like a credential: don't post it publicly, don't paste it into analytics-laden tools. URL fragments aren't sent in HTTP requests, but they do land in browser history, extensions, and clipboard.
    • Use a fine-grained PAT scoped to Gists only (Account permissions → Gists: Read and write). If the link leaks, revoke the PAT at github.com/settings/tokens — blast radius is limited to gists on your account.
    • Do not store sensitive PII (SSNs, account numbers, tenant personal info). Put it somewhere with real access controls.
    • Use Settings → Export regularly to keep a local JSON backup.
