IV.
Caveats & Externalities
Read before acting

A report of this sort is only as honest as its admissions. The figures above are assembled from rules of thumb and publicly available heuristics — no address-specific data was retrieved, and every estimate deserves independent verification before money moves. What follows is a list of places where pro-forma arithmetic routinely fails to match reality.

i.Value estimates are rough — verify before acting

  • The purchase price is a neighborhood midpoint guess for 95207 based on typical SFH characteristics. Pull actual comps (Redfin / Zillow / MLS) within 0.5 mi, past 90 days, matching bed/bath/sqft.
  • Rent assumes a 3BR/2BA SFH in typical condition. Verify via Rentometer, Zillow Rent Zestimate, and at least five active listings of comparable units in 95207.
  • Interior condition is not factored in. Deferred maintenance can swing rent ±15% and CapEx needs dramatically.
  • This tool assumes a traditional long-term lease. Short- and mid-term rental math is entirely different — higher gross, much higher expenses, and material regulatory risk.

ii.California & Stockton specifics

  • AB 1482 (statewide rent cap): Likely applies if the property is more than fifteen years old. Caps rent increases at 5% + CPI, maximum 10%/yr. Just-cause eviction rules apply.
  • Stockton local ordinances: Stockton has entertained tenant-protection proposals; check current municipal code before underwriting aggressive rent growth.
  • Prop 13 reassessment: A sale triggers reassessment at the purchase price. The seller's current tax bill is not what you will pay. Model at ~1.1–1.3% of your purchase price.
  • Mello-Roos / special assessments: Some Stockton parcels carry CFD bonds adding $1,000–$4,000/yr on top of base property tax. Pull the full tax bill before closing.
  • Insurance availability: California's landlord insurance market is constrained. Several carriers have paused new policies. Get a binding quote early; the FAIR Plan is a backup, but expensive and limited.
  • Flood / levee risk: Stockton sits in the Delta. Check FEMA flood zone for the parcel; levee-protected areas may still face reclassification.
  • Seismic: Earthquake insurance is separate (CEA). Not included in the baseline insurance figure.
  • Property tax timing: California taxes are due 12/10 and 4/10. A supplemental tax bill often arrives after purchase and catches new investors off guard.

iii.Operating cost blind spots

  • Capital expenditures are lumpy: A $12,000 roof or $8,000 HVAC replacement hits in a single year. The CapEx reserve line smooths this on paper only.
  • Eviction cost: A non-paying tenant in California can take 3–6 months and $5,000–$15,000 in legal and lost rent to remove. Not modeled.
  • Bad-debt loss: Vacancy covers empty units, not tenants who stop paying. Add 1–2% for realistic collection loss.
  • Trash-out and make-ready: Post-eviction or heavy-use tenant turns can cost $3,000–$10,000+.
  • Landlord insurance deductibles: Often $2,500–$5,000. Small claims come out of pocket.
  • Utilities during vacancy: You pay water, gas, and electric minimums when no tenant is in place.

iv.Financing & transaction caveats

  • Investment-property loans require 20–25% down minimum, price in a 0.5–0.75-point rate premium versus owner-occupied, and have stricter DTI and reserve requirements.
  • Closing cost estimate excludes prepaid tax and insurance impounds — those add 2–6 months of each at closing.
  • Buying in an LLC usually means commercial-loan terms (higher rate, shorter amortization, balloon) instead of a 30-year fixed.
  • Depreciation (27.5-yr SL on building basis) is not modeled here but materially improves after-tax returns.
  • This report shows pre-tax cash flow. Actual after-tax return depends on your marginal rate, depreciation, passive-loss rules, and whether you qualify as a real estate professional.

v.Market & macro risks

  • Stockton was the epicenter of the 2008 crash — prices fell approximately 65%. The market can be volatile. Do not underwrite to aggressive appreciation.
  • Central Valley rents track Bay Area commuter demand and Amazon / logistics employment. Remote-work unwind and warehouse-sector softness can compress rents.
  • The interest-rate environment affects both your refinance optionality and future exit cap rates.
  • Property taxes grow at Prop 13's 2%/yr cap on assessed value, but insurance and maintenance can grow faster than rent in any given year.

vi.What this report does not include

  • Formal inspection, appraisal, title search, or survey.
  • Environmental review (underground tanks, lead paint for pre-1978 construction, asbestos, radon, methamphetamine contamination).
  • Rent-control status verification for this specific parcel.
  • School district quality, crime statistics, walk scores, or neighborhood trajectory.
  • Legal or tax advice. Consult a California real estate attorney and CPA before purchasing.

vii.Data sources to verify

  • Comparable sales: Redfin, Zillow, MLS (via agent), PropertyRadar
  • Comparable rents: Zillow Rent Zestimate, Rentometer, Apartments.com, Craigslist active listings
  • Tax bill: San Joaquin County Assessor-Recorder (sjgov.org)
  • Flood zone: FEMA Flood Map Service Center (msc.fema.gov)
  • Rent law: California Department of Real Estate + local Stockton municipal code
  • Insurance quote: Bind with a CA-admitted carrier; don't rely on national averages
