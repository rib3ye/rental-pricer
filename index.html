Caveats & externalities

Value estimates are rough — verify before acting

  • Purchase price shown is a neighborhood midpoint guess for 95207 based on typical SFH comps. Pull actual comps (Redfin/Zillow/MLS) within 0.5 mi, past 90 days, same bed/bath/sqft.
  • Rent estimate assumes a 3BR/2BA SFH in typical condition. Verify via Rentometer, Zillow Rent Zestimate, and 5+ active listings of comparable units in 95207.
  • No interior condition data is factored in. Deferred maintenance can swing rent ±15% and CapEx needs dramatically.
  • This tool assumes a traditional long-term lease. Short-term/mid-term rental math is entirely different (higher gross, much higher expenses, regulatory risk).

California / Stockton-specific risks

  • AB 1482 (statewide rent cap): Likely applies if the property is >15 years old. Caps rent increases at 5% + CPI, max 10%/yr. Just-cause eviction rules apply.
  • Stockton local ordinances: Stockton has had tenant-protection proposals; check current municipal code before underwriting aggressive rent growth.
  • Prop 13 reassessment: Purchase triggers reassessment at sale price. Seller's current tax bill is NOT what you'll pay. Model at ~1.1–1.3% of your purchase price.
  • Mello-Roos / special assessments: Some Stockton parcels carry CFD bonds adding $1,000–$4,000/yr. Pull the tax bill before closing.
  • Insurance availability: CA homeowner/landlord insurance market is constrained. Several carriers have paused new policies. Get a binding quote early; FAIR Plan is backup but expensive and limited.
  • Flood / levee risk: Stockton sits in the Delta. Check FEMA flood zone for this parcel; levee-protected areas may still require flood insurance or face reclassification.
  • Seismic: Earthquake insurance is separate (CEA). Not included in baseline insurance figure.
  • Property tax timing: CA taxes are due 12/10 and 4/10. Supplemental tax bill after purchase can catch new investors off guard.

Operating cost blind spots

  • Capital expenditures are lumpy: A $12k roof or $8k HVAC replacement hits in one year, not spread evenly. The CapEx reserve line smooths this on paper only.
  • Eviction cost: A non-paying tenant in CA can take 3–6 months and $5k–$15k in legal/lost rent to remove. Not included.
  • Bad-debt loss: Vacancy % covers empty units, not tenants who stop paying. Add 1–2% for realistic collection loss.
  • Trash-out and make-ready: Post-eviction or heavy-use tenant turns can cost $3k–$10k+.
  • Landlord insurance deductibles: Often $2,500–$5,000. Small claims come out of pocket.
  • Utilities during vacancy: You pay water/gas/electric minimums when no tenant is in place.

Financing & transaction caveats

  • Investment-property loans require 20–25% down minimum, price in 0.5–0.75% rate premium vs. owner-occupied, and have stricter DTI/reserve requirements.
  • Closing cost estimate excludes prepaid tax/insurance impounds — those add 2–6 months of each at closing.
  • If buying in an LLC, expect commercial-loan terms (higher rate, shorter amortization, balloon) instead of 30-yr fixed.
  • Depreciation (27.5-yr SL on building basis) is not modeled here but materially improves after-tax returns.
  • This tool shows pre-tax cash flow. Your actual after-tax return depends on your marginal rate, depreciation, passive-loss rules, and whether you qualify as a real estate professional.

Market & macro risks

  • Stockton was the epicenter of the 2008 crash — prices fell ~65%. The market can be volatile. Don't underwrite to aggressive appreciation.
  • Central Valley rents track Bay Area commuter demand and Amazon/logistics employment. Remote-work unwind and warehouse-sector softness can compress rents.
  • Interest-rate environment affects both your refinance optionality and future exit cap rates.
  • Property taxes grow at Prop 13's 2%/yr cap for the assessed value, but insurance and maintenance can grow faster than rent in any given year.

What this report does NOT include

  • Formal inspection, appraisal, title search, or survey.
  • Environmental review (underground tanks, lead paint for pre-1978, asbestos, radon, meth contamination).
  • Rent-control status verification for this specific parcel.
  • School district quality, crime stats, walk scores, or neighborhood trajectory — all of which affect tenant quality and long-term rent growth.
  • Legal/tax advice. Consult a CA real estate attorney and CPA before purchasing.
